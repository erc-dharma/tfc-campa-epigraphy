    Apparatus

    A12 899 ◇ 1033

    Translation by Arlo Griffiths and Salomé Pichon

    A1-A3[Śaka year] 897. This is the snaḥ of the men who offered fields at that time:

    • the prince (pu lyaṅ) Śrī Jayasiṅhavarman,
    • with prince (pu lki) Kumāramātya, chief (kluñ) of the city,
    • with my lord chief (pov ku kluñ) Kunurun,
    • with Satyādhipati,
    • with lord (pov) Saṅgrāmacara,
    • with Sabhādharmmādhikāra,
    • with lord (pov) Jayavikrama,
    • with lord (pov) Yudhādhikāra,
    • with my lord (pov ku) Nyāyādhipati,
    • with Raṁgyāv Hrey Atāl,
    • with Atuk, child of lord chief (pov kluñ) Jrāy,
    • with Pvah Dat.

    A3-A5These are the fields at the Luvu vihāra. Its limit (gauy): the tanaṅgoh (starting point? landmark?) is the border (? tupen) of Pandanus trees. It goes until above the vihāra. From above the vihāra, it goes along the great road. la panuc the main road. It goes until the luvu above lām̃. It goes from there and falls (lac) until the river of the Luvu vihāra. It closely (rai sīr) that river back to the border (? tupen) of Pandanus trees.

    A5-A8Inside that section (? manāṅ) there are no other fields at all [besides] those fields Kachutra and Ganan, the fields of the Y.P.K. Śrī Hrinaṇḍaliṅgeśvara. These fields are for becoming transplantations: 393 plots, [and] for pajāk: their contents is one hundred and fifty-one jāk tandoṅ. Also those sowing [fields] on the side: 390 plots, their contents is 186 jāk tandoṅ.

    A8-A12This is the field Vvair in Tanah Kandaic. Its limits: from the tanaṅgoh (formed by) the border of Tāc (trees?), it goes straight/flat (? maAndap) to the East. It goes along the dike shared with the field of the God Varadeveśvara and also until kajec, it falls to the main road. It turns and goes straight/flat to the South until it shares one dike with the field Śihvan Mavera. It closely follows that [field] going straight/flat to the West back to the border of Tāc. Inside [that] section (? manāṅ) there are no other kachutra fields @@@at all@@@ [besides] this field and the field that is the field of the Y.P.K. Śrī Hrinaṇḍaliṅgeśvara . The transplantation fields: 69 plots, for pajāk its content is 25 jāk 9 v↓1 tandoṅ. Those sowing [fields] on the side: 18 plots, their contents is 25 jāk tandoṅ.

    A12-A16In [the year of the] Śaka king 899, day 11 of the waxing [fortnight] of month 9, Friday. That was the time when po ku kluñ Panilih carried away (surak) the statue of Śrīpāda Parameśvara pu pov kuv until/to it reaches/up to the ājñā lyam̃ po ku Ulīk, high king of Pāndar , to offer fields. In the country of Ulīk: the field Carāv (starling, blackbird) , the field Paliy Anaiḥ (small village), the field Vatek and the field Dauk Śirā (reside at the top?) and the field Janam̃ḥ Avista (all janam̃ḥ). These fields of the Śrīpāda Parameśvara pu pov kuv who by royal favor gave already to the ājñā lyam̃ po ku Śrīmahāsāmanta. In Atāla, he gave to the yām̃ Ājñā lyam̃ po ku Balik to offer these fields to become the fields of the yām̃ po ku Śrī hrinaṇḍaliṅgeśvara (d)iga Śri God of Campa.

    A17-This is the snaḥ of the noblemen and of the common men who will offer these fields at that time to the Ājñā lyam̃ po ku Ulīk, high king of A(g)yam̃ who is the chief of the city

    Commentary

    A1-A3In my translation, I normalise words and titles of Sanskrit’s origin according to Sanskrit spelling. Many cases involved spelling without final -a of Sanskrit words that end with this letter. In some instances, one notes that the engraver himself was inconsistent, notably in the toponyms - Atāl, also spelled Atāla - kachuta, also spelled kachutra

    A8L. A11, ce qui semble être une abbréviation suivant le nombre se rencontre à nouveau. Corriger ou laisser le “k” comme tel?
