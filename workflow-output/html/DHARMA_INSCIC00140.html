Apparatus

A12 899 ◇ 1033

Translation by Arlo Griffiths and Salomé Pichon

A1-A3[Śaka year] 897. This is the snaḥ of the men who offered fields at that time:

  • the prince (pu lyaṅ) Śrī Jayasiṅhavarman,
  • with prince (pu lki) Kumāramātya, chief (kluñ) of the city,
  • with my lord chief (pov ku kluñ) Kunurun,
  • with Satyādhipati,
  • with lord (pov) Saṅgrāmacara,
  • with Sabhādharmmādhikāra,
  • with lord (pov) Jayavikrama,
  • with lord (pov) Yudhādhikāra,
  • with my lord (pov ku) Nyāyādhipati,
  • with Raṁgyāv Hrey Atāl,
  • with Atuk, child of lord chief (pov kluñ) Jrāy,
  • with Pvah Dat.

A3-A5These are the fields at the Luvu vihāra. Its limits (gauy): the tanaṅgoh (starting point? landmark?) is the border (? tupen) of Pandanus trees. It goes until above the vihāra. From above the vihāra, it goes along the great road. la panuc the great road. It goes until the luvu above lām̃. It goes from there and falls (lac) until the river of the Luvu vihāra. It goes along that river closeby until the border (? tupen) of Pandanus trees again.

A5-A8Inside that manāṅ Upak other field Kachuta and the Ganan field that are the fields of the Y.P.K. Śrī Hrinaṇḍaliṅgeśvara, the fields which then will be transplanted [on] 393 galauk, for its pajāk harvest of one hundred and fifty-one jāk. Slaves sowing coconut tree that sip [on] 390 galauk, its harvest of 186 jāk (per? with?) slaves.

A8-A12This is the field Ravver in Tanaḥ Kandaic. Its limits: from the tanaṅgoḥ border of Tāc, going ahead (? maAndap·) to the East it goes to one dike with the field of the yām̃ Vardeveśvara and also until karajac, it falls to the great road. It turns and goes ahead to the South until one dike with the field Śihvan Mavera, it follows that going ahead to the West it goes closeby until the border of Tāc again, in manāṅ the Upak field Vukan Kachuta. And the field that is the field of the Y.P.K. Śrī Hrinaṇḍaliṅgeśvara will then be a field transplanted on 69 galauk, for its pajāk harvest by 25k 9v slaves sowing. Next to that, 18 galauk (which are) harvest by 25k slaves.

Commentary

A1-A3In my translation, I normalise words and titles of Sanskrit’s origin according to Sanskrit spelling. Many cases involved spelling without final -a of Sanskrit words that end with this letter. In some instances, one notes that the engraver himself was inconsistent, notably in the toponyms - Atāl, also spelled Atāla - kachuta, also spelled kachutra
